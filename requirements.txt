To impress the judges, your frontend needs to look like a piece of High-Performance Industrial HMI (Human-Machine Interface) software. It should not look like a standard "website." It needs to scream efficiency, speed, and clarity. Since you have no live feed, your UI must communicate state effectively so the user never feels "blind" while the camera captures and processes. 1. The Core Concept: "Kiosk Mode" vs. "Dashboard Mode" Don't try to cram everything onto one screen. Use a Dual-Mode Architecture: * The Inspection Station (Kiosk Mode): This is where the worker spends 99% of their time. It is distraction-free, high-contrast, and designed for rapid repetition. * The Operator Dashboard (Slide-out/Secondary): This is for checking stats and submitting daily reports. 2. The Inspection Station Flow (Kiosk Mode) Since you are using a relay-based flash, the user experience is "Trigger -> Flash -> Result." Here is the suggested state flow: State A: The "Ready" Screen (Idle) * Visual: A massive, pulsating button in the center labeled "SCAN IC" (or physical spacebar trigger). * Visual Guide: A static, high-quality outline or semi-transparent image of a "perfect" IC overlaying the center. This tells the worker exactly where to place the chip since there is no live video feed. * Instruction: "Place IC in Jig & Press Scan." State B: The "Flash" Sequence (Critical for UX) * The Problem: Without a live feed, the screen feels dead between the click and the result. * The Solution: Fake the "Flash" on the UI. * User clicks "Scan." * UI Action: The screen flashes white for 100ms (simulating the physical flash). * Audio: Play a mechanical "shutter" sound (satisfying feedback). * Transition: Immediately show a "Processing..." loader. Do not just show a spinning circle. Show a "Scanning..." animation (e.g., a laser line moving over a chip icon) to imply intelligent analysis is happening. State C: The "Verdict" Screen (The Money Shot) * Design: Split the screen 50/50. * Left: The photo you just took. Overlay bounding boxes on the detected text/defects (Green box for good text, Red box for scratches/anomalies). * Right: A massive, unambiguous Status Card. * GENUINE: Bright Green Background. Large Checkmark Icon. Text: "PASS / GENUINE". * FAKE: High-Contrast Red Background. Warning Icon. Text: "FAIL / COUNTERFEIT". * Reason: "Reason: Texture Anomaly (Confidence: 98%)" or "Reason: Wrong Part Number." * Call to Action: A big "NEXT SCAN" button (auto-focused so they can just hit Enter). 3. Navigation & Worker Dashboard Recommendation: Use a Collapsible Sidebar (Left) for the Worker Dashboard. * Why? The inspection screen must be full-width to maximize the image detail. The dashboard is secondary. * Interaction: A "Hamburger" menu icon in the top-left corner toggles the sidebar. Sidebar Content (The "Worker Dashboard") When the worker opens the sidebar, they see their "Shift Stats" without leaving the inspection page. * Profile Card: Photo, Name, Shift ID. * Shift Stats (Live): * Total Scanned: 142 * Pass Rate: 94% * Fakes Found: 3 (Make this a clickable number to see a gallery of today's fakes). * Tools: * "Report Issue" (Broken Jig, Lighting failure). * "End Shift / Submit Report." The "Daily Report" Page This should be a separate page (or a modal) triggered from the sidebar. * Auto-Fill: The software knows what they did. Don't make them type numbers. * Auto-filled: Date, Time, Operator Name, Total Scanned, Pass/Fail Count. * User Input: * Anomalies Note: "Lot #45 had consistently bad printing." * Jig Condition: "Okay" / "Needs Cleaning." * Submit Button: Sends a JSON payload to your Supervisor Dashboard. 4. Visual Design "Cheat Sheet" for Judges To make it look "High Impression" (Industrial & Professional): * Dark Mode Default: Reduces eye strain for operators working long shifts. Use a dark slate grey (#1e293b) background, not pitch black. * Color Coding (Accessibility Standard): * Pass: Emerald Green (#10b981). * Fail: Rose Red (#f43f5e). * Action Buttons: Blue/Primary (#3b82f6). * Typography: Use a Monospaced Font (like Roboto Mono or JetBrains Mono) for all data (Part Numbers, Lot Codes, Confidence Scores). It looks technical and engineered. 5. Summary of Pages/Views You essentially need 3 Views for the Worker Frontend: * Inspection View (Main): The Kiosk (Ready -> Scan -> Result). * Gallery View (Pop-up): "Show me the last 10 scans" (useful if they accidentally skipped one). * End-of-Shift Report View: Simple form for submission. Pro-Tip for the Demo: Add a "Demo Mode" toggle in the settings. When active, pressing scan doesn't actually trigger the relay/camera but instantly loads a pre-saved "Fake" or "Genuine" image sequence. This ensures your presentation is flawless even if the hardware glitches on stage.
